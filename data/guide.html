Supervisor Responsibilities

  1. To be familiar with the use of the IT Facilities provided by NUSSU commIT
  2. To assist users with the use of the IT Facilities provided by NUSSU commIT
  3. To be proactive when assisting users
  4. To be present at the computer center during their duty
    1. To inform Ops if otherwise
  5. To maintain the working conditions of IT Facilities provided by NUSSU commIT
  6. To enforce the rules and regulations of the computer center
    1. Food and drinks are not allowed, with the exception of water in enclosed containers
    2. Noise that might disturb other users are not allowed, this includes (but not limited to):
      3. Group discussions
      Playing audio from the computers
  7. To uphold the professional image of NUSSU commIT and maintain a high spirit of service at all times
  8. To be contactable
  9. To be familiar with the demerit system described this document
  10. To submit their notice of withdrawal from NUSSU commIT 2 weeks prior to their termination date.

Standard Operating Procedures

  1. All Supervisors of NUSSU commIT are allowed a maximum of 16 hours of duty per week during term time and 42 hours per week during holidays
  2. Be punctual for duty. Supervisors are required to arrive at their respective duty venue at least 5 minutes before the start of their duty.
    1. A buffer of 15 minutes is allowed after the start of the duty, provided the supervisor has informed Ops beforehand.
    2. The previous supervisor is to attempt to contact the next supervisor if the latter does not come 5 minutes within the start of duty. Previous supervisor to inform Ops afterwards.
  3. Supervisors must not leave their duty venue for more than 15 minutes during a standard, 2 hour duty period.
    1. If a supervisor has an extended duty period of equal to or greater than 4 hours, and those hours fall within the lunch period of 11 to 3 and dinner period of 5 to 9, supervisors are allowed to leave the duty venue for lunch or dinner break. The duration of the break shall not exceed 30 minutes.
  4. Supervisors are to leave their contact details on their duty venues when they leave the duty venue
  5. Supervisors are not allowed to sleep during their duty period
  6. Supervisors to sign in through the log book and to fill in their claim forms.
    1. Failure to do so may result in the loss of duty money for these time period.
  7. Supervisors are not allowed to drink in the center (with the exception of water) at all times.
  8. Supervisors shall wear their duty shirt during their duty period.
    1. A spare duty shirt is made available for supervisors if they require one.
  9. Members are only allowed access to cupboard 1 of YIH (to check EZ Link value) and Ops green cupboard (to borrow a duty shirt).
  10. Access to other parts of the cupboard is allowed, ONLY with the explicit permission for an authorized person(s).
  11. Off duty supervisors are not allowed to enter the supervisor desk area
    1. The Managing Committee is exempted from this policy.
  12. To follow the proper procedure when changing toners, as trained by Ops.
  13. To follow proper procedure when handling lost and found, as trained by Ops
  14. Mutual swapping of duties can be done amongst supervisors, but must be reflected clearly on the duty website.
  15. Supervisors are not allowed to fraternize with users during their duty period
  16. Supervisors are allowed to leave 5 minutes before the end of their duty.
    1. Leave the contact details of the next supervisor before leaving.

Opening and Closing of Center

  • YIH
Opening
  1. Switch on center lights and air conditioning
  2. Switch on release station monitors.
  3. Ensure center keys are present in the Operations tray
  4. Obtain the Reprint EZ Link and verify amount inside the card
  5. Prepare printers to operating conditions.
  6. Ensure adequate paper on A3 and A4 printers.
    7. -Put paper to tray for A3 printer.
    -Press the big back arrow button
  7. Sign in to the Supervisor Sign In/Out book and fill in the Claim Form.
    8. -Supervisor shall verify that the last recorded value in the Supervisor Sign In/Out Book tallies with the Reprint EZ Link value.
    -If number does not tally, report to Ops.
  8. Ensure there is adequate stapler bullets. Top up as necessary.
    -Three to five stapler bullets for each size.
  9. Open blinds on the center window
  10. Flip the CLOSED sign towards OPEN on the center door.
  11. Open Center Two
    12. -Switch on Center Two lights and air conditioning
    -Flip the CLOSED sign towards OPEN on the center door.
  12. Check for any items left on the tables and in the scanners for both Centre One and Centre Two.
    13. -Follow proper Lost and Found procedures if any items found.

Closing
  1. Pop − up window on computers will warn users that computers will shut down 15 minutes prior to the center closing time.
  2. Switch off release station monitors.
  3. Unload paper from A3 printer and put it on top of the printer.
  4. Count the number of remaining paper boxes and reams.
  5. Count the number of remaining B/W printer toner.
  6. Sign in to the duty website and post the EOD.
  7. Check for any items left on the tables and in the scanners.
    8. -Follow proper Lost and Found procedures if any items found.
  8. Close Center Two
    9. -Switch off Center Two lights and air conditioning
    -Flip the OPEN sign towards CLOSED on the center door.
  9. Close blinds on the Center One window.
  10. Flip the OPEN sign towards CLOSED on the center door
  11. Switch off lights and air conditioning.
  12. Lock Center One door

Printing Problem

  1. Printer is not available on the list
    1. Go to user computer
    2. Open 'Run' program (Shortcut Win + R)
    3. Type in \\172.18.37.210
    4. Right click and connect to the appropriate printer
  2. Printing multiple slides in one page
    1. Use Adobe PDF Reader
    2. Select Multiple
    3. Set the correct settings including selecting the correct printer
    4. Let user click print(Supervisors are not allowed to click the print button for the users. This is to safeguard the supervisors in case there are any printing setting mistakes.)
  3. Error message
    1. If an error message appears on the screen after clicking print button, i.e. there is no selected pages to be printed, that is a file error and the supervisor cannot do anything for that.
    2. Tell the user to try to print from the original file, if there is.
  4. Print job cannot appear on the release monitor
    1. Check whether the ID that is typed in the release monitor is correct.
    2. Check whether the print job is sent to the correct printer.
    3. Check whether the file is printed directly from the Internet.
      4. -The file from the Internet should always be downloaded first before printing.
    4. Check whether the file is spooling (waiting for uploading) or not.
      1. Go to \\172.18.37.210
      2. Double click on the corresponding printer
      3. There will be a pop up screen listing down all the print job
      4. Click on the owner tab to sort out all the users' ID
      5. Find the corresponding user ID
      6. Check the status of the print job, it should be paused
        7. -If it is paused, then go to the release monitor to try again
        -If it is still spooling, just wait until the status changes to pause. Usually this is because the file is too large, hence it takes some time for the file to be uploaded.
  5. User claims that the print job cannot be printed out
    1. Ask the users whether they have paid.
    2. Go to the release monitor, put the cursor on the most left side of the screen, press 'Alt+Q' to check the print history.
    3. Look out for user ID and the filename that they print.
      1. If it is not there, it is highly likely that they have not paid. Ask the users to key in their ID again and lookout for the print job. It should still be there.
      2. If it is there, the users have paid. Check the printer whether there is any error and solve accordingly.
        3. If there is nothing wrong with the printer, contact Campus Supply
  6. Re-printing
    1. Re-printing is allowed if print result differs from the file and setting of the user.
    2. Verify that fault in printing is not caused by user
      1. Check settings that it is indeed multiple
      2. Check that print job is sent to correct printer
      3. Check that user document is clean
      4. Check that the file is not printed directly from the Internet
    3. If verified that is indeed problem with the printers, INFORM OPS before issuing a re-printing form
      1. Campus Supplies is open
        1. Obtain re-printing form
        2. Go to release station and point mouse to top left. Press Alt + Q to access print log
        3. Verify number of printed pages and write down in the re-printing form.
        4. Fill in the rest of the necessary details.
        5. Staple together faulty print job and write down reason for re-print on the re-printing form.
        6. Send user to Campus Supplies for re-printing.
      2. Campus supplies is closed
        1. Check the print log (mouse at top left and press Alt + Q)
        2. Record the necessary details in the Reprinting Record Book
        3. Reprint the user's document using the Reprinting EZ Link Card
    4. Obtaining an Official Receipt
      1. Obtain 'Unofficial Printing Receipt' form
      2. Fill in receipt
      3. Direct user to Campus Supplies to obtain official receipt by handing in the unofficial receipt
